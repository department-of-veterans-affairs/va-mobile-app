Voice and tone‚Äã

We follow the VA design system content guidelines on voice and tone. The guidelines emphasize talking to users person-to-person, being empathetic, and using a conversational voice when communicating with users. Since many of our features are health related we follow the VA content guidelines around health content by also writing clear, plain language content.

Accessible and inclusive language‚Äã

Our app is for all Veterans. This means that we write for everyone and use language that does not exclude or harm any of our users. We avoid using ableist language such as the words ‚Äúview‚Äù or ‚Äúsee‚Äù as they implicate sight. We also write with respect to the community by using people first language. We also avoid using words like impairment.

Resources to follow:

  • Gov UK inclusive communications
  • Georgetown University best practices guide
  • Microsoft guideline

Grammar‚Äã

We follow VA design system content rules regarding grammar with the following adjustments:

Capitalization‚Äã

  • Screen names (e.g. Home, Profile, Vaccine Records) are title case. With the new navigation design we will be moving to sentence case for screen titles.
  • All other headings in the app are sentence case.

Times and time zones‚Äã

  • We currently use AM and PM as this is default formatting in React Native.

Naming‚Äã

We generally follow VA design rules for naming forms and labels.

When naming components in our design library we use the Sketch format: Group-name/Symbol-name. When applicable, we also include subgroup names between the group name and symbol name.
